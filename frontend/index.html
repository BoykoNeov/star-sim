Powerful stellar winds have stripped this massive star's outer layers — a process the track does model — baring its hot, helium- and carbon-burning core: a Wolf–Rayet star. Scrub to watch the surface peel from hydrogen to helium (WN) to carbon and oxygen (WC/WO) as the bare core climbs toward ~250,000 K. The 3D star wraps the blazing core in an evocative optically-thick wind — the colour is the true blackbody at Teff, but the streaming halo is illustrative, not a measured mass-loss rate. The spectrum panel shows the real wind-emission spectrum from PoWR models — broad He II and nitrogen lines on the helium (WN) surface, carbon lines once it turns to carbon (WC) — wherever the star maps onto the grids; once the bared core is hotter and more compact than any observed Wolf–Rayet, it honestly shows no model.
